How To Use Obliger

Obliger helps groups stay on top of shared responsibilities and track completion together.

At Work

  • Restocking supplies
  • Checking in guests
  • Resetting rooms between events

At Home

  • Household chores
  • Feeding pets
  • Giving medications

In the Community

  • Event setup
  • Carpool rotations
  • Checking on neighbors

Quick Start

  1. Join a CircleGot an invite code? Tap Join Circle on the Circles tab and enter it. No code? Tap Create Circle to start your own and invite others.
  2. See what needs doingYour Circle's shared tasks are on the Tasks tab. Tap any task to open it.
  3. Check in when you do oneOpen a task and tap Mark Done. Everyone in the Circle sees it's handled.
  4. Set your reminder timeObliger nudges you once a day about what's due. Pick the hour in Settings.
Good to know: Obliger sends reminders once a day, at the single time you choose in Settings -- not at a specific time per task. If a task is due, it shows up in that day's reminder. There's no separate alarm clock for each task.

Logging In

Login

Enter your email address and password and tap Sign In. If you don't have an account yet, tap Sign Up to create one. If you've forgotten your password, tap Forgot Password.

Create Account

Enter your email address, a display name, and a password. Your display name is the default name other Circle members will see -- you can change it later in Settings, and you can also set a different name for each Circle you join. Tap Create Account to finish. You'll be signed in immediately.

Forgot Password

Enter your email address and tap Send Code. You'll receive an email containing a verification code. Enter that code on the next screen to set a new password.

If you don't see the email, check your spam folder. When you find it, enter the code manually rather than tapping any link in the email -- some email apps consume the link automatically before you can use it.

Password Reset

Enter the verification code from your email, then enter and confirm your new password. Tap Reset Password. You'll be signed in with your new password immediately.

Tasks

Task List

The Task List is the main screen of Obliger. It shows all tasks across your Circles, with overdue and upcoming tasks sorted by due date so the most urgent items appear first.

Use the Circle selector at the top to filter tasks down to a single Circle. Tap any task to open its detail.

Tap the + button to add a new task to the currently selected Circle.

Task Detail

The Task Detail screen shows everything about a task and is where you take action on it.

Details card. Shows the task name, which Circle it belongs to, its due date (highlighted if overdue or due today), and any notes. Two action buttons appear at the bottom of this card:

  • Mark In Process / Back to Not Started -- toggles the task between Not Started and In Process. Use In Process to let other Circle members know you're actively working on it.
  • Mark Done -- logs the task as complete with your name and the current time. For recurring tasks, the due date automatically advances to the next occurrence. For one-time tasks, the task is closed.

Owner. Shows who owns the task. If you are the owner, a Change Owner button appears. Tap it to transfer ownership to any other member of the Circle. The previous owner stays in the Circle as a regular member. Only the owner can edit the task, unless the owner has allowed all members to edit it.

Recurrence card (recurring tasks only). Shows the recurrence schedule and the next due date. An End Recurrence button appears at the bottom of this card. Tapping it permanently stops the task from repeating after a confirmation prompt -- this cannot be undone.

History. A full log of every completion appears at the bottom of the screen, showing who marked the task done and when.

If you are the task owner, or if the owner has allowed all members to edit, an Edit button appears at the top right. If you are not the owner and editing is not enabled, the task is read-only.

New Task

Tap the + button on the Task List to open this screen. Fill in:

  • Name -- required. What is this task?
  • Circle -- which Circle this task belongs to. Defaults to the Circle currently selected in the Task List.
  • Recurrence -- how often the task repeats. Set to one-time if it only needs to happen once.
  • Due Date -- when the task is first due.
  • Notes -- optional. Any additional context members need to know.
  • Allow all members to edit -- when enabled, any Circle member can edit this task. When disabled, only you (the owner) can edit it.
  • Notifications -- a list of all Circle members. Check the members who should receive push notifications when this task is due. All members are checked by default. A toggle below the list controls whether new members who join the Circle later are automatically added to the notification list.

Tap Save. The task appears immediately on the Task List.

Edit Task

Contains the same fields as New Task. Accessible from the Task Detail screen if you are the owner, or if the owner has allowed all members to edit. Changes take effect immediately on save.

If editing is not enabled and you are not the owner, all fields appear grayed out and cannot be changed.

Circles

Circle List

Shows all Circles you belong to, sorted alphabetically. Tap any Circle to open its detail. Two buttons at the top let you create a new Circle or join an existing one with an invite code.

Circle Detail

Shows the full picture of a Circle and its members.

Join Code. Every Circle has a unique invite code. A Copy Code button copies it to your clipboard so you can share it however you like.

Owner. Shows who owns the Circle. If you are the owner, a Change Owner button appears. Tap it to select any other Circle member as the new owner. The previous owner stays in the Circle as a regular member.

Members. A list of everyone in the Circle, shown by their display name for that Circle.

Invite Someone. Opens the Invite Someone screen where you can share the join code or send an email invitation.

If you are the Circle owner, an Edit button appears at the top right, allowing you to rename the Circle. Only the owner can edit the Circle name.

New Circle

Enter a name for your Circle and tap Create. You'll be taken directly to the Circle Detail screen. You are automatically the owner, and you'll appear in the Circle under your app display name. Share the join code with people you want to add.

Join Circle

Enter the invite code shared with you by a Circle owner or member. Before joining, you'll be asked to choose the display name you want to use in that Circle. It defaults to your app display name, but you can set it to anything you like -- useful when different groups know you by different names.

Your per-Circle display name is set at join time and applies only to that Circle. Changing your app display name in Settings later will not change the name you chose for a Circle you've already joined.

Invite Someone

Shows the Circle name and its join code. Two options for sharing:

  • Copy Code -- copies the join code to your clipboard. Paste it into a text message, chat, or anywhere else.
  • Share via Email -- opens a pre-populated email in your default mail app with the Circle name and join code ready to send. Just add the recipient and tap Send.

Anyone who receives the code can join by opening Obliger, tapping Join Circle on the Circles tab, and entering the code. They'll need an Obliger account to join.

Edit Circle

Accessible from the Circle Detail screen for Circle owners only. Change the Circle name and tap Save. The new name appears immediately everywhere in the app. Only the Circle owner can access this screen.

Settings

Settings

Profile. Your display name and email are shown and editable inline. Tap either field to edit it directly on the screen. A Save button appears in the header when you make a change.

  • Your display name here is your default name. It pre-fills the name prompt when you join a new Circle, but changing it does not affect Circles you've already joined.
  • Email changes require confirmation. After saving, check your new email address for a confirmation link. Your email will not update until you tap that link.

Notification Time. The single hour of day when Obliger sends your daily reminder about tasks that are due. Each person sets their own notification time -- your preference does not affect other Circle members. This is one time for all your tasks, not a separate time per task.

Appearance. Toggle between light and dark mode, or follow your device's system setting.

Change Password

Sends a verification code to your current email address. Enter the code on the Password Reset screen, then set your new password. You do not need to know your current password to complete this -- only access to your email is required.

Delete Account

Permanently and irreversibly deletes your Obliger account. This screen requires two confirmations before anything is deleted.

When you delete your account, the following is permanently removed:

  • Your account and profile
  • All Circles you created
  • All tasks you created
  • All completion history associated with your tasks

Members of your Circles will immediately lose access to those Circles and their task history. There is no recovery option. If you want to hand off your Circles or tasks before leaving, use Change Owner on each Circle and task first.
